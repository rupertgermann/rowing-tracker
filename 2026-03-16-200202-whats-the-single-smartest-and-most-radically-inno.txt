  Adaptive Power Curve & Race Strategy Engine

  The single highest-leverage addition: a physiological performance model built
  from the stroke-by-stroke data you're already collecting but barely
  exploiting.

  Why this is the smartest move

  You have stroke-level power, pace, and heart rate data for every session —
  that's an incredibly rich dataset sitting largely untapped. Right now the
  analytics are retrospective ("here's what happened"). This feature makes them
  predictive ("here's what you can do, and how to do it").

  No SmartRow app does this. It's the kind of feature that makes users feel the
  app knows them.

  What it consists of

  1. Critical Power / Power-Duration Curve
  - Adapts cycling's well-established Critical Power model to rowing
  - Scans all stroke data to find best-effort power at every duration (10s, 30s,
   1min, 2min, 5min, 10min, 30min, 60min)
  - Fits a hyperbolic curve: P(t) = CP + W' / t where CP = aerobic threshold, W'
   = anaerobic capacity
  - Visualized as an interactive power-duration curve chart
  - Updates automatically as new sessions are imported
  - Shows how the curve shifts over weeks/months (fitness trajectory)

  2. Race Strategy Simulator
  - User picks a target distance (e.g., 2000m) and optional goal time
  - Engine generates an optimal pacing plan based on their power curve — not
  generic advice
  - Shows split-by-split targets (per 100m or 500m) with power, pace, stroke
  rate
  - Compares negative split, even split, and positive split strategies with
  predicted finish times for each
  - "What-if" mode: drag sliders to adjust first-500m effort and see cascading
  effects on remaining splits
  - Overlay against their actual past attempts at that distance

  3. PR Prediction with Confidence Intervals
  - Uses power curve trend + training load trajectory to predict achievable PRs
  - For each standard distance: "You can likely hit X:XX within Y weeks if you
  maintain current training"
  - Confidence bands (50% / 80% / 95%) so it feels scientific, not hand-wavy
  - Identifies which factor is the bottleneck (aerobic base vs anaerobic
  capacity vs technique/efficiency)

  4. Training Zone Calculator
  - Derives personalized power zones from the CP model (not generic percentages)
  - Zone 1 (Recovery) through Zone 6 (Neuromuscular) with exact watt ranges
  - Retroactively tags every past session with time-in-zone distribution
  - Feeds directly into the existing training plan system: "Your plan says
  'endurance' — that means 145-175W for you"

  Architecture sketch

  The key insight

  The power curve model is deterministic math, not AI — it's reliable,
  explainable, and instant. But it integrates beautifully with the existing AI
  coach: the chat can reference the user's CP, W', zones, and predicted PRs to
  give advice grounded in their actual physiology rather than generic rowing
  wisdom.
